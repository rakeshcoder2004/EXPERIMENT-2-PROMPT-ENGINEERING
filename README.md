# EXP-2-PROMPT-ENGINEERING-

## Aim: 
Comparative Analysis of different types of Prompting patterns and explain with Various Test Scenarios

Experiment:
Test and compare how different pattern models respond to various prompts (broad or unstructured) versus basic prompts (clearer and more refined) across multiple scenarios. 
Analyze the quality, accuracy, and depth of the generated responses.


## Algorithm:
Introduction
Artificial Intelligence chatbots rely on prompting techniques to understand user instructions and generate meaningful responses. 
Different prompting strategies influence the quality, reasoning ability, and accuracy of outputs generated by AI systems. 
This document compares four major prompting techniques—Zero‑Shot, Few‑Shot, Chain‑of‑Thought, and Role‑Based prompting—across three popular AI platforms: Google Gemini, Microsoft Copilot, and ChatGPT.
Example Passage Used for Comparison

## Output
```
Prepared by: ABINAYA
REGISTOR NUMBER:25014594/212225230003


Artificial Intelligence is rapidly transforming industries such as healthcare, education, finance, and transportation. 
AI systems help automate tasks, improve efficiency, and support decision‑making. However, the widespread adoption of AI also raises concerns about job displacement, data privacy, and ethical use of technology.
Task given to the AI systems: Summarize the passage in one or two sentences.
1. Zero‑Shot Prompting
Definition:
Zero‑shot prompting refers to giving an AI model a task without providing any examples. 
The model relies entirely on its pre‑trained knowledge to complete the task.
Example Prompt:
Summarize the following passage in one sentence.
Effectiveness:
Gemini – Provides concise summaries.
Copilot – Produces clear productivity‑focused responses.
ChatGPT – Produces well‑structured summaries with balanced information.
Advantages:
• Fast and simple
• Requires minimal prompt design
• Useful for quick tasks
Limitations:
• Output format may vary
• Less accurate for complex reasoning tasks

2. Few‑Shot Prompting
Definition:
Few‑shot prompting provides a few examples before the actual task to guide the AI model about the expected format and style.
Example Prompt Structure:
Example 1:
Text: AI helps doctors detect diseases early.
Summary: AI improves healthcare diagnosis.
Example 2:
Text: Robots increase production speed in factories.
Summary: Robots improve manufacturing productivity.
Now summarize the main passage.
Effectiveness:
Gemini – Generates more consistent summaries.
Copilot – Produces clear formatting similar to given examples.
ChatGPT – Produces highly structured and consistent responses.
Advantages:
• Improves accuracy
• Controls format and tone
• Reduces ambiguity
Limitations:
• Requires additional prompt preparation
• Longer prompts

3. Chain‑of‑Thought Prompting
Definition:
Chain‑of‑Thought prompting encourages the AI to think step‑by‑step before producing the final answer.
Example Prompt:
Read the passage carefully, identify the key points step by step, and then provide a summary.
Effectiveness:
Gemini – Provides logical reasoning steps.
Copilot – Explains the analysis clearly before summarizing.
ChatGPT – Produces the most detailed reasoning process.
Advantages:
• Improves logical reasoning
• Useful for complex analytical tasks
• Helps reduce incorrect responses
Limitations:
• Longer responses
• Slightly slower processing time

4. Role‑Based Prompting
Definition:
Role‑based prompting instructs the AI to behave like a specific professional such as a teacher, journalist, or academic researcher.
Example Prompt:
You are an academic researcher. Summarize the passage in a formal academic tone.
Effectiveness:
Gemini – Produces professional tone.
Copilot – Generates business‑style writing.
ChatGPT – Produces highly structured academic summaries.
Advantages:
• Controls tone and writing style
• Useful for professional communication
• Improves context awareness
Limitations:
• Requires clear role instructions

Overall Comparison
Zero‑Shot Prompting:
Best for simple tasks that require quick answers.
Few‑Shot Prompting:
Improves consistency and accuracy by guiding the AI with examples.
Chain‑of‑Thought Prompting:
Most effective for reasoning and analytical tasks.
Role‑Based Prompting:
Best for controlling tone and perspective in writing tasks.
Platform Performance:
ChatGPT generally performs best in reasoning and structured responses.
Google Gemini provides fast and concise answers.
Microsoft Copilot performs well for professional and productivity‑oriented tasks.
```

## Result
Prompting techniques play a crucial role in determining the effectiveness of AI responses. 
Among the techniques studied, Chain‑of‑Thought and Few‑Shot prompting often produce the most accurate and structured outputs. 
Each AI platform demonstrates strengths depending on the type of task, but all benefit significantly from well‑designed prompts.
Understanding and applying the right prompting technique can greatly improve the quality, clarity, and usefulness of AI‑generated responses.
